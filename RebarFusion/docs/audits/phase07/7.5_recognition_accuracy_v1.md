# Phase 7.5 — Recognition Accuracy Audit

Scope: `core/recognition/recognizers.py` (8 recognizers), evaluated over all 651 connected components of `SS-GF-01(M).dxf`. This audit assumes Phase 7's *determinism* is already fixed and regression-locked (`audit_phase07_determinism.md`) and asks the separate question: **is the output correct?**

Methodology: recognized every component with every individual recognizer (not just the winning one) to capture full evidence; cross-referenced each label against its source DXF layer(s) and edge geometry (as an independent ground-truth proxy, since only `S-RBAR` is the structural rebar layer — everything else is architecture/annotation); rendered grid galleries of samples per label/layer combination and visually inspected them; where a numeric pattern was suspicious, checked it across the *entire* population, not just the sample. Full dataset: `debug/phase07_5/recognition_audit_dataset.json`. Galleries: `debug/phase07_5/gallery_*.png`.

## Headline result

**None of the 6 shape recognizers (`StraightBarRecognizer`, `LBarRecognizer`, `UBarRecognizer`, `ClosedShapeRecognizer`, `BranchRecognizer`, `StructuralOutlineRecognizer`) check what layer a component is on**, and none validate angles, aspect ratio, or symbol-like regularity — only `DimensionRecognizer` and `LeaderRecognizer` check layer. They classify purely on edge-count + node-degree topology. That is enough for the simplest shape (a single line) but is not enough to tell a rebar bend from an annotation tick, a leader arrowhead, a north-arrow symbol, or a bar drawn in double-line width convention. The result: **precision varies enormously by label**, from ~82% (straight bars) down to essentially 0-20% for three of the six shape classes.

## Confidence is not a real per-instance signal

Every recognizer emits one of six **fixed constants** regardless of the specific instance: `straight_bar`=0.95, `l_bar`/`u_bar`/`dimension`=0.90, `branch`=0.95, `structural_outline`=0.86, `stirrup`=0.85, `leader`=0.80. The confidence histogram is just the label distribution re-bucketed by these constants — it carries **zero within-label discriminating power**. Two `u_bar` components, one a genuine bent bar and one a straight bar's double-line width artifact, both report confidence 0.90. A confidence-threshold cutoff (as originally requested) is not actionable until confidence is computed per-instance (see Recommendations).

## Per-label findings (evidence-based, most important first)

### `u_bar` — 79 components, estimated true precision ≤11%
**91% (72/79) share the exact edge-length triple `[420.0, 420.0, 8.0]`** — two long parallel edges of identical length plus one short (8-unit) end cap. Visually (gallery `gallery_u_bar_S-RBAR.png`, `gallery_u_bar_thin_sliver.png`) these render as a thin sliver: two nearly-touching parallel lines representing a **single straight bar drawn in double-line width convention**, with one end capped — not a bent U-shaped bar at all, which should have a wide flat/curved base and two legs at scale with the bend. `UBarRecognizer` matches purely on "3 edges, 2 nodes degree-1, 2 nodes degree-2" — this exact pattern is what a double-line-with-one-cap straight bar also produces.

### `stirrup` — 40 components, estimated true precision ~20%
**80% (32/40) are regular pentagons — all 5 edges ≈9.4 units, equal within measurement noise.** Visually (`gallery_stirrup_S-RBAR.png`) these render as a symmetric "home plate"/arrow pentagon, not a rectangular rebar tie. This is almost certainly a recurring CAD symbol (marker/callout/north-arrow) that happens to be a closed 5-edge loop, which is all `ClosedShapeRecognizer` checks for (`edges >= 4 and is_loop`). Of the remaining 8, 4 are simple 6×22 rectangles (plausible genuine small ties) and 4 are more complex multi-edge loops not yet individually confirmed.

### `branch` — 68 components, estimated true precision ≤12%
Only 8/68 (12%) are on the `S-RBAR` layer at all. The single largest class, **24/68 (35%), is on `G-ANNO-TEXT`** and visually (`gallery_branch_G-ANNO-TEXT.png`) is unambiguously a **leader arrowhead** (one long shaft + two short barbs meeting at a point — exactly the "degree ≥3 node" pattern `BranchRecognizer` looks for). Another 20/68 are on `A-GENM` (general annotation), unconfirmed visually but same non-structural-layer pattern.

### `l_bar` — 67 components, estimated true precision ~63%
42/67 (63%) are on `S-RBAR` and visually (`gallery_l_bar_S-RBAR.png`) look like genuine hooked/bent bars — a long leg with a short bent kick near one end. This is the one shape-recognizer result that held up well. But **20/67 (30%) are on `A-DETL-MBND` alone** and visually (`gallery_l_bar_A-DETL-MBND_only.png`) are clearly **bend-mark / callout tick symbols** (a long horizontal leader plus a short vertical tick), not physical bars. A further 5/67 are on `A-FLOR`, same non-structural pattern.

### `structural_outline` — 3 components, estimated true precision ~0%
All 3 are on `A-FLOR` (architectural floor plan), 0% on `S-RBAR`. `StructuralOutlineRecognizer` (large closed loop, >10 edges) is almost certainly picking up the room/floor outline itself, not a rebar layout boundary. Sample size is small but the layer signal is unambiguous.

### `straight_bar` — 332 components, estimated true precision ~82%
273/332 (82%) are on `S-RBAR` and visually (`gallery_straight_bar_S-RBAR.png`) are simple, unambiguous single lines — this is the best-performing recognizer, likely because "one line" is nearly impossible to confuse structurally. The remaining 59/332 (18%) are on non-structural layers (`A-FLOR`, `A-GENM`, `G-ANNO-SYMB`, `A-DETL-MBND`, `G-ANNO-DIMS`, `A-FLOR-HDLN`, `A-FLOR-OVHD`) and visually (`gallery_straight_bar_nonS-RBAR.png`) are indistinguishable plain architectural/detail lines — false positives, just fewer of them because most non-structural single lines don't exist in isolation as their own connected component as often as multi-edge annotation shapes do.

## Aggregate false-positive estimate

Using "component includes zero `S-RBAR` edges" as a conservative lower-bound false-positive proxy (a real rebar component could theoretically have zero S-RBAR edges if mis-layered, and a non-S-RBAR component could coincidentally be geometrically valid, but neither is likely at scale): **151 of 589 non-unknown shape labels (25.6%) have no S-RBAR presence at all**, and visual inspection confirms these are genuine misclassifications, not edge cases. Within the nominally "on-layer" remainder, `u_bar` and `stirrup` still show majority misclassification once shape (not just layer) is checked — so **the true false-positive rate is meaningfully higher than 25.6%**, concentrated in `u_bar` (~89% wrong) and `stirrup` (~80% wrong).

## Unknown breakdown (62 components)

**41/62 (66%) are single-ARC components on the `S-RBAR` layer**, each ~9.7-9.8 units long. This is not noise — it's a single, well-defined, missing capability: **no recognizer handles a standalone arc**, which is exactly how a standard rebar hook bend is often drawn. `StraightBarRecognizer` explicitly excludes `ARC` edge type (correctly — an arc isn't straight), but nothing else picks it up, so every hook-only component falls through to `unknown`. This is the highest-value gap to close, not because unknowns are "bad" but because it's a real, common, and completely well-defined rebar feature (a hook) currently invisible to the pipeline.

The remaining 21 unknowns are open (non-closed) chains ranging from 2 to 25 edges — plausibly genuine complex/overlapping geometry or multi-segment annotation chains. Lower priority; needs case-by-case review, not a systemic fix.

## Recommendations (prioritized by impact)

1. **Add layer gating to all 6 shape recognizers** (require `S-RBAR` membership, or majority-S-RBAR for mixed components). This alone eliminates the `l_bar`/`A-DETL-MBND`, `branch`/`G-ANNO-TEXT`+`A-GENM`, and `structural_outline`/`A-FLOR` false-positive classes — the single highest-leverage fix.
2. **Add a double-line/end-cap rejection filter to `UBarRecognizer`** (and check `ClosedShapeRecognizer` for the analogous 4-edge thin-rectangle case): reject shapes where two edges are near-parallel, near-equal length, and much longer than the remaining edge(s) — that pattern is a bar-width representation, not a bend.
3. **Add a regular-polygon rejection filter to `ClosedShapeRecognizer`**: reject near-regular polygons (all edge lengths within a tight tolerance) — real stirrups/ties are rectangular or hook-shaped, not symmetric symbols.
4. **Add a hook/arc-end recognizer** for standalone-ARC `S-RBAR` components — closes 66% of the current `unknown` bucket with a well-defined, common rebar feature.
5. **Make confidence genuinely per-instance** (incorporate layer-match strength and shape-fit residual) so a future confidence threshold is actually meaningful, rather than being a fixed constant that just re-encodes which recognizer fired.
6. **Resolve a latent tie-break risk**: `LBarRecognizer` (0.90, registered 2nd) and `DimensionRecognizer` (0.90, registered 7th) can tie; `RecognizerRegistry.evaluate()` uses strict `>` so the first-registered recognizer wins ties. A 2-edge component on a `DIM` layer would currently be classified `l_bar`, not `dimension`. Should be resolved by registration-order review or explicit layer-based priority once fix #1 is in place.

## Verdict

Phase 7 is deterministic (confirmed in `audit_phase07_determinism.md`) but **not yet accurate**. `straight_bar` (the majority label, 51% of components) is reliable at ~82% precision. The other five shape labels range from ~63% (`l_bar`) down to ~0-20% (`u_bar`, `stirrup`, `structural_outline`), driven almost entirely by the complete absence of layer and shape validation. This is exactly the risk the determinism work was meant to surface before trusting anything downstream: Phase 8 (annotation association) and Phase 9 (engineering families) currently consume this recognition output directly, so a `u_bar` misclassification would propagate as a wrong bar shape into a wrong engineering family and wrong reconstruction, with no error raised anywhere. **Do not proceed to Phase 8 on top of current recognition output without at minimum fix #1 (layer gating)** — it is the cheapest fix here and removes the largest share of confirmed false positives.
