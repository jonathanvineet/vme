# Phase 7.6 — Physical Plausibility Engine

Implements the reopened-Phase-7 fix as a separate evidence layer, per your direction: recognition (`core/recognition/recognizers.py`) is untouched — it still answers "what shape is this" from topology alone. A new stage, `core/recognition/plausibility.py`, answers "could this reasonably be a real piece of reinforcement" and attaches evidence + a decision without overwriting the recognizer's label or confidence.

## Design
No fixed length cutoff. Two independent rules, both derived from the drawing's own data per recognition label (not a hardcoded number):
- **MinimumLengthRule** — modified z-score (median + MAD), Iglewicz & Hoaglin's standard robust-statistics convention. Reject below z=-3.5, review below z=-2.5.
- **RelativeScaleRule** — length as a fraction of the label's own median. Reject below 5%, review below 15%.

Combined by taking the more skeptical of the two per component. Labels with fewer than 5 same-label components in the drawing are accepted without judgement (not enough data to build a distribution) rather than guessed at.

## Why two rules, not one
Built the z-score rule first, ran it, and it caught **zero** of the known-bad components — including the exact N4 case that motivated this stage. Traced why rather than declaring success: `straight_bar` lengths in this drawing span from near-zero to several meters, so MAD is large (~103mm), and a 0.47mm fragment only scores z=-1.29 — nowhere near the -3.5 threshold, despite being obviously not a real bar. Pure z-score under-detects near-zero outliers against a population with wide natural spread. Added the relative-scale rule (ratio to median) as a complementary check that catches "near zero relative to typical," which the z-score alone missed.

## Honest limitations found, not hidden
- **`l_bar`**: 19 of 42 components (45%) sit at exactly 5.34mm — the same artifact type as the original N4 discovery. These land in **"review," not "reject"**: their ratio to the label median (89.28mm) is 5.98%, just above the 5% reject cutoff. I did not retune the threshold to force these into "reject" — that would be fitting the rule to this one drawing's exact numbers rather than keeping it principled. They are correctly flagged as suspicious and excluded from nothing yet, visible for human review.
- **`stirrup`**: only 4 components in the drawing — below the 5-sample minimum, so all are auto-accepted without a plausibility judgement. Known limitation given the small population; not fixable without either lowering the sample-size floor (weakening the "trustworthy" bar) or gathering more data.
- Both limitations are transparent in the per-component evidence, not silently absorbed into "accept."

## Wiring
`EngineeringAssociationEngine` (`core/engineering/association.py`) now takes an optional `plausibility` map; components with a `reject` decision are excluded from association candidacy (same mechanism as the existing `symbol`/`unknown` exclusions). `review`-decision components are **not** excluded — only `reject` is confident enough to remove from candidacy, matching the accept/review/reject trichotomy rather than collapsing review into a binary cut. Wired into both `run_phase8.py` and `run_phase9.py`. New standalone `run_phase7_6.py` (matching the existing per-phase runner pattern) for direct inspection, dumping `debug/phase07_6/<drawing>/physical_plausibility.json`.

```

After:  bar-shape components recognized: 334, plausibility-rejected: 31, review: 39, accepted: 264
```
Every rejection is explainable from `debug/phase07_6/<drawing>/physical_plausibility.json` (length, label median, both rule scores). Not every implausible component was caught — `l_bar`'s 19-component cluster and `stirrup`'s small population are documented, known residual gaps, not silently swept under "accept." This is the same "known, bounded, documented ambiguity" standard used throughout this audit (Phase 6 duplicate edges, Phase 7.5 residual gaps) rather than a claim of 100% coverage.

## Recommendation
This stage is usable now — it materially reduced contamination (31 confirmed-implausible components removed from candidacy, 39 more flagged for review) without touching the recognizer or hardcoding a drawing-specific number. The `l_bar` near-miss is worth a future look (possibly a per-label review-ratio threshold tuned by evidence from more drawings, once available, rather than this single one), but is not urgent — those 19 components are visible in the review bucket, not silently trusted.
