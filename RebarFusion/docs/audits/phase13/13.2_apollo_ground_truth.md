# Phase 13.2 — Apollo Ground Truth (first real benchmark corpus entry)

**No pipeline code was modified. No benchmark framework code was modified** (one exception disclosed below: none — the framework ran as frozen). This document records the methodology, evidence, results, and open questions for the corpus's first real project.

## Status of the labels, stated first

These labels are a **draft prepared for engineer verification**, not verified ground truth. `metadata.json` says so explicitly (`labeled_by: "Claude (AI assistant) — DRAFT … PENDING VERIFICATION"`, `engineer_hours: 0`). The benchmark's numbers below therefore measure *pipeline agreement with a careful, provenance-tracked draft reading of the drawings* — a meaningful but weaker claim than agreement with engineer-verified truth. The draft exists to cut the engineer's task from "label a package from scratch" to "verify and correct a documented draft."

## Steps 2-4 — Labels and provenance

Every fact's provenance is in its `notes` field; the evidence hierarchy (`notes.md`): visual PDF reading > raw DXF text (ezdxf, independent of Phases 3-12) > **never pipeline output** (circular). Facts that could not be proven are `null` with a stated reason — notably: SS-GF-01's N4/N6/N7 diameters (no resolving schedule exists in the package), all N↔T cross-sheet mappings (deliberately unasserted), and PW-GF-09 T16 quantity (A17). ## Step 7 — A17 investigation

New evidence gathered (raw text positions + observation bboxes, no pipeline modification):

- `2 -T16` appears **8 times** on PW-GF-09(R), in two sheet regions (x≈-28.7k/-27.9k and x≈-21k/-22k).
- The two merged observations are **22mm-long section-view symbols** (bar cross-section markers, not drawn bars), ~570mm apart, **each with its own `2-T16` annotation**, plus a third T16 observation (19 tiny members near two more labels) that the accepted identity does *not* include.
- Two separately-labeled locations leans toward **distinct physical bar groups** (the accept would then be a false merge); the alternative — one pair of edge bars labeled at both faces of a section — cannot be excluded without engineer-level reading of the section geometry.

**A17 remains open.** `docs/engineering_assumptions.md` was *not* updated (the evidence does not resolve it); `docs/known_limitations.md` was updated with the sharpened evidence. The question is now precisely framed for an engineer: *are the two `2-T16`-labeled cross-section positions at (-28469, 3711) and (-27861, 3697) on PW-GF-09(R) the same two bars or two different pairs?*

## Newly discovered limitations (documented, not fixed)

1. **Same-mark selector granularity** (schema v1): `{drawing, mark}` cannot distinguish PW-GF-09(R)'s five T8 functional groups; GT is one record per (element, mark) with the functional breakdown in `families.json`. Physical-bar-level labels need a schema extension (engineer-legible selectors — e.g. by functional label text or sheet region), to be designed when an engineer actually labels at that level.
2. **Mould-sheet reference codes produce almost no observations** (the Recognition/Association bucket above): dimension-chain N-codes with no adjacent bar geometry never become families, so anything whose only appearance is a mould dimension chain is invisible to Phase 12. Known in outline since the research report; now measured.
3. **The pipeline's N4 `diameter=8.0` is now formally contradicted by a GT label** (`gt-ss01-n4`) rather than just suspected — first candidate for the Phase 12.1 flag's promised tracing, *when* an algorithm phase reopens.
