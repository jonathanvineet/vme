Every bar's count/length/weight is reconciled against the (S) schedule per mark, not just per diameter: solid bars are real geometry measured from the (R) elevation, matched to a schedule row by length. Dense-mesh crossing fragmentation means detection alone misses ~40-60% of the true schedule weight on most panels (measured directly, see geo_reconcile.py) — the disclosed shortfall for each mark is filled with a synthesized bar of the schedule's exact length, placed near a real sibling of the same mark where one was found, or evenly across the panel otherwise. Translucent/dimmer bars marked "~" in the bar list are these synthesized ones (including all bent/U-bar and other multi-segment shapes, which drawing geometry can't reliably locate at all — indistinguishable from crossing-bar noise) — shape, count and weight are exact, position is estimated. Front/back depth (Z) measured from the drawing's own section-cut circles where available (no evidence nearby = left at mid-thickness). Depth exaggerated ×6 — real wall thickness is only ~150mm. Green pipes = sleeves/corrugated conduit, real measured position and diameter from the drawing's own arc geometry. Corbel/anchor cast-in hardware checked directly in the mould (M) drawings — found drawn only in a schematic detail area disconnected from true position, so not shown (no reliable location data exists).
